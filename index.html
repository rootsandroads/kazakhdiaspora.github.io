Survey Insight 01

Why do Kazakh students choose to study abroad?

Behind every departure is more than a diploma.

Click to reveal insight →
Survey Insight 01

According to the Roots & Roads survey:

Most respondents did not choose to study abroad solely for university rankings or prestige. The strongest motivations were better career prospects, access to higher-quality education, cultural experience, and international exposure. A significant number also pointed to limited opportunities in their home country.

Insight: Studying abroad is rarely an escape. For many Kazakh students, it is a deliberate step toward growth, independence, and broader professional horizons — not a rejection of home, but an expansion of possibility.

Survey Insight 02

What students feared most before leaving

Excitement often comes with quiet doubts.

Click to reveal insight →
Survey Insight 02

According to the Roots & Roads survey:

Before applying, students most frequently reported concerns about financial burden, academic pressure, language barriers, homesickness, and visa or legal uncertainty. These fears appeared consistently across different countries and study levels.

Insight: Fear is not an individual weakness — it is a shared starting point. Almost every student begins their journey with uncertainty, yet these concerns rarely determine outcomes. They mark the beginning of adaptation.

Survey Insight 03

What students actually struggled with abroad

The reality is more complex than expectations.

Click to reveal insight →
Survey Insight 03

According to the Roots & Roads survey:

Students most often described challenges related to loneliness, financial stress, cultural adjustment, academic pressure, and emotional exhaustion. Many emphasized that the most difficult part was not studying itself, but learning to live independently far from family support.

Insight: Life abroad builds resilience before success. Growth often happens through everyday struggles that are rarely visible, but deeply transformative.

Survey Insight 04

Would students choose this path again?

After everything they experienced.

Click to reveal insight →
Survey Insight 04

According to the Roots & Roads survey:

The majority of respondents indicated they would definitely or probably choose to study abroad again. Overall satisfaction levels were high, even among students who reported significant challenges.

Insight: Studying abroad is not perfect — but it is meaningful. For most students, the independence, perspective, and personal growth gained outweigh the difficulties they faced along the way.
